Events Information

01

The Adoption of the Constitution

Date : 26thNovember1949

The Constitution of India was adopted by the Constituent Assembly on this date. This is a significant event for the legislative framework as it marks the formal creation of the structure of government, including the Parliament (Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha). It laid the foundation for the laws and governance structure in India. It is celebrated as Constitution Day or Samvidhan Diwas every year.

02

The Basic Structure Doctrine

Date : 24thApril1973

The Supreme Court of India, in the landmark case Kesavananda Bharati vs. State of Kerala, propounded the Basic Structure Doctrine. The judgment established that while the Parliament has the power to amend the Constitution, it cannot alter its "basic structure." This doctrine ensures judicial protection against excessive amendments, safeguarding the balance of power in the Indian Constitution.

03

Proclamation of Emergency

Date : 25thJune1975

The Emergency declared by Prime Minister Indira Gandhi is one of the most significant events in India's constitutional history. The period from 1975 to 1977 saw the suspension of civil liberties and fundamental rights. The powers of the Executive were significantly expanded during this time, impacting the balance between the three branches. This event is crucial for understanding the limits and checks on the Executive's power.
